Chocobo Deckbuilding Journey
Concept Sorting & Refinement Curve Adjustments Final Tweaks & Playtesting

1. Initial Concept & Theme

This deck started as a Chocobo concept but evolved into a Toski-led nostalgia-driven, meme-heavy deck focusing on old-school cards and silly creatures.

I pulled cards that caught my eye, divided them into creatures and non-creatures, and then organized them by mana cost.

2. Sorting & Refinement

As I pulled cards, I found more that were meme-worthy in SparkJoy than the seven I initially planned. That led me to consider Toski as the commander, letting me play more fun cards rather than optimizing a Voltron-style Chocobo deck.

I started sorting by asking myself which cards truly sparked joy and which ones fulfilled the deck’s vision. I also noticed the distinction between cards that leverage multiple creatures versus those that boost a single creature.

3. Mana Curve & Playability Adjustments

Noticing an overloaded 3-drop slot, I made cuts to smooth out the curve. I focused on adding more 1- and 2-drop creatures and ramp to accelerate Toski.

Some spells, like Shamanic Revelation, became unnecessary because Toski naturally provides repeatable card draw. Similarly, I cut high-cost enchantment removal since I had lower-cost options.

When recounting, I realized I forgot to count my proxied Chocobo! That was an injustice that had to be fixed.

4. Final Refinements & Playtesting

I reviewed my cuts and added creatures over spells to stay on theme. After fine-tuning the deck, I locked in the land count and prepared to test.

First Game Night

On my first night out with this deck, I got to see an old friend from five years ago. I played Joven’s Ferrets on turn three and beat face with them all game. Toski kept the cards flowing, and Nykthos gave me a boatload of mana.

The game’s turning point: My opponent cast Jokulhaups, wiping the board. But I had two open mana for Heroic Intervention. I saved my board and won from there.

This deck’s synergy, fun factor, and resilience all came together in its first real game. A perfect first run!
